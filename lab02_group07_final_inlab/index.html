Formulae I love

Quadratic formula
There are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0, a\ne0\) and they are $$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.$$
Gaussian Distribution
$$f(x) = {1 \over {\sqrt{2\sigma\pi}}} e^{-(x-\mu)^2\over{2\sigma^2}} $$
Important integration formula
$$\int_0^x {1\over{\sqrt{x^2+a^2}}} = \log \bigg| {x+\sqrt{x^2+a^2} \over{a}}\bigg|$$
Modified Ampere's law
$$\oint {\vec{B}.\vec{dl}} = \mu_0(I + \epsilon_0{d\phi_B\over{dt}})$$
